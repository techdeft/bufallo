Businesses & Economic Sectors in Buffalo Narrows
Buffalo Narrows has a growing and diverse economy driven by natural resources, tourism, transportation, local services, construction, and Indigenous and community partnerships. The community serves as an important northern service hub in Northwest Saskatchewan.

Key economic sectors include tourism, mining support services, forestry, fishing, wild rice harvesting, transportation, retail trade, hospitality, construction, and government services.

Major Economic Sectors
Tourism & Hospitality
Tourism is one of the largest economic drivers in Buffalo Narrows due to its lakes, fishing opportunities, beaches, outdoor recreation, and northern scenery.

Businesses in this sector include:

The Buffalo Lake House

Churchill Lake Guesthouse

Janelle's Room Rentals

SCREAMING EAGLE OUTFITTERS

Pelican Tavern

The nearby Buffalo Narrows Sand Dunes Park and Kisis Channel are also major tourism attractions.

Mining & Resource Development
Northern Saskatchewan’s mining industry — particularly uranium development — creates opportunities for local transportation, construction, camp services, logistics, and Indigenous partnerships.

This sector presents opportunities in:

Heavy equipment services

Transportation and logistics

Construction and maintenance

Camp catering and accommodations

Workforce development

Indigenous business partnerships

Forestry, Fishing & Wild Rice Harvesting
Traditional resource-based industries remain important to the region’s economy. Forestry, commercial fishing, trapping, and wild rice harvesting continue to provide employment and business opportunities.

Transportation & Logistics
Due to its northern location, Buffalo Narrows plays an important role in regional transportation and logistics. Highway access, air transportation, and northern hauling services support tourism, mining, and local supply chains.

The community is served by:

Highway 155

Buffalo Narrows Airport

Float plane/water aerodrome access
